# Romance and investment scams

## Romance scams
The pattern: weeks of warm, attentive messages from someone you have never met
in person — often claiming to work abroad — followed by an emergency only your
money can solve, or an "investment opportunity" they want to share. Never send
money to someone you have not met in person, and be suspicious of anyone who
always has a reason a video call is impossible.

## Crypto and investment bait
Guaranteed returns do not exist. Celebrity giveaways that "double your
Bitcoin", trading platforms your online friend insists you try, and investment
groups showing screenshots of huge profits are all variations of the same
scam. Money sent to a crypto address is gone — there is no bank to call and no
refund process.

## Advance-fee fraud
Any offer where you must first pay a small fee — for taxes, customs,
processing, or account activation — to unlock a much larger sum is fraud. The
large sum never existed. This covers lottery winnings you never entered,
unclaimed inheritances from unknown relatives, and generous strangers who
chose you for a donation.
