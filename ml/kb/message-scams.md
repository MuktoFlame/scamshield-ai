# Common message scams

## Fake bank alerts
Banks never ask you to "verify your identity", share a PIN, or click a link to
unlock an account by text message. If a message claims your account is locked,
call the number printed on your bank card — never the number or link in the
message itself.

## Gift cards are money
No legitimate business, government agency, or employer settles anything in
gift cards. The moment anyone asks to be paid in iTunes, Google Play, or Steam
cards, it is a scam — gift card codes are untraceable cash that cannot be
refunded.

## The family emergency trick
A message claiming to be a relative in urgent trouble on a new number, asking
for secrecy and fast money, is one of the oldest scams. Hang up and call the
relative on the number you already have. A real emergency survives a two-minute
check; a scam does not.

## One-time passwords (OTP)
An OTP code is the key to your account. Banks and companies send codes TO you;
they never call or message to ask you to read a code back. Anyone asking for
your OTP — no matter who they claim to be — is breaking into your account at
that very moment.
