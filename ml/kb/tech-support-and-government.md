# Tech support and government impersonation

## Fake tech support
Microsoft, Apple and Google do not call people about viruses. A pop-up with a
phone number, or a caller asking to install remote-access software (AnyDesk,
TeamViewer) so they can "fix" your computer, is a scam. Hang up and close the
browser. If someone did get remote access, disconnect the device from the
internet and have it checked before using online banking again.

## Government and tax threats
Tax agencies and the police do not threaten arrest by phone, and they never
demand payment through gift cards, wire transfers or cryptocurrency. Official
matters arrive by letter, and no genuine agency needs to be paid within the
hour. Threats of a "warrant" that can be canceled by instant payment are
always fraudulent.

## Utility disconnection threats
"Your electricity will be cut TODAY unless you pay immediately" is a pressure
script. Utility companies send multiple written notices before any
disconnection. Check your account directly on the provider's official app or
website, or call the number on a past bill.
