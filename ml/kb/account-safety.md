# Everyday account safety

## Passwords
Use a different password for every important account — email above all, since
whoever controls your email can reset everything else. A password manager, or
even a paper notebook stored at home, beats reusing one password everywhere.

## Two-factor authentication
Turning on two-factor authentication means a stolen password alone is not
enough to enter your account. Enable it for email, banking, and social media
first. App-based codes are stronger than SMS codes, but any second factor is
far better than none.

## Checking a login alert
"New sign-in to your account" alerts are faked constantly. Never use the
button in the alert itself — open the app or type the website address
yourself, then check the account's own security page for real sessions.

## Public Wi-Fi and shared devices
Avoid logging into banking on public Wi-Fi or someone else's device. If you
must, change the password afterwards from your own device.
