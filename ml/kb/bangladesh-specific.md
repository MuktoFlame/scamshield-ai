# Scams in Bangladesh: bKash, Nagad and local patterns

## Mobile money (bKash/Nagad) scams
The classic script: a call or SMS claims your bKash or Nagad account will be
suspended, or that you received money "by mistake" that must be returned. The
caller then asks for your PIN or the OTP sent to your phone. bKash and Nagad
never call to ask for a PIN or OTP — sharing them hands your wallet to the
caller. Dial the official helpline (bKash: 16247, Nagad: 16167) yourself if
unsure.

## Fake job offers
Offers of easy online work paying hundreds of taka per task, requiring a
"registration fee" or "training kit" payment first, are advance-fee fraud.
Legitimate employers pay you — never the other way around.

## Where to report
Report online fraud to the National Emergency Service by dialing 999, or to
the Police Cyber Support for Women & Children and the Cyber Police Centre
(cyber@police.gov.bd). Keep screenshots of messages, numbers, and transaction
IDs — they make investigation possible.

## Lottery and prize SMS in Bangla
Prize messages claiming you won a lottery from a telecom operator or TV show,
asking for a fee or your mobile-money PIN to claim, are mass-sent scams. No
operator awards prizes that require payment to receive.
