# Safe online shopping

## Prices that are too good
A flagship phone at a fifth of its price is not a bargain — it is bait. Before
buying, search the product's normal price. Counterfeit sellers rely on the
excitement of a deal overriding the obvious question of why nobody else sells
it that cheap.

## Stay inside the platform
Marketplace buyer protection only works when payment goes through the
platform's own checkout. A seller who asks to move to WhatsApp or Telegram,
requests a bank transfer, mobile money, or gift cards, or offers a discount
for paying "directly" is removing your only safety net on purpose.

## Judging a seller
Look at the account's age, past listings, and review patterns. Dozens of
five-star reviews posted in the same week, all in the same generic wording
("best product ever", "100% original"), are purchased reviews. A genuine
seller history grows unevenly over months and includes mundane complaints.

## Counterfeit warning signs
Blurry or watermarked stock photos, brand names slightly misspelled, "OEM" or
"replica" wording buried in the description, and shipping from an unexpected
country for a local listing all point to counterfeit goods.
