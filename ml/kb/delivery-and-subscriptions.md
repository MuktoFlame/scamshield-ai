# Delivery and subscription scams

## Fake delivery notices
"Your package could not be delivered — pay a small customs/redelivery fee" is
bait for your card details; the tiny fee is never the point. Track parcels
only inside the courier's official app or by typing the courier's website
address yourself. Couriers do not collect fees through links in text messages.

## Fake subscription renewals
Emails claiming Netflix, Amazon or an antivirus charged you hundreds of
dollars, with a phone number to call for a "refund", are designed to get you
on the phone with the scammer. Check your actual bank statement and the
service's own app; if no charge exists, the email is the scam.

## Fake order confirmations
An invoice for an expensive item you never bought, with a number to call to
"cancel the order", works the same way: the danger is the phone call, where
you will be talked into remote access or a "refund" that empties your account.
